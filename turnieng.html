Part II, Alan Turing

The Turing machine
A Turing Machine is a simple theoretical machine invented by Alan Turing in 1936. It is used to understand how computers solve problems. The machine has: a long tape divided into squares, a head that can read and write symbols on the tape, a set of rules that tells it what to do. The machine works step by step. It reads a symbol, follows a rule, writes a symbol if needed, and moves left or right.

it is very important in artificial intelligence because it helps us understand what problems machines can solve and what they cannot do.

The Turing test
The Turing test, proposed in 1950, is an ability test to find out if a machine can behave in an intelligent way. The test includes a person, the machine which is being tested, and a judge. The person and the machine have to chat, meanwhile the judge must understand who is the person, if the judge can't understand who is person the machine is considered intelligent.
